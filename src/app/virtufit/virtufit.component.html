Start with Style: Personalize Your Outfit Journey

Let's dive into the 1st screen of VirtuFit. Users can select profiles they're shopping for, personalise recommendations and save outfits. It offers:

  • Trendy collections and past outfits for style inspiration
  • Occasion-based outfit choices for diverse needs
  • A quick outfit search for swift selections.

        This design was based on what I learned from my research, which highlighted that users have diverse shopping preferences and sometimes seek swift, hassle-free outfit selection.

Discover Outfits Your Way

In VirtuFit, the user has the freedom to experience their chosen outfit in three unique ways:

  • Detailed View: Get an up-close and comprehensive look at the outfit.
  • Quick Overview: Easily access essential information about the outfit.
  • Immersive Try-On: Immersive Try-On: Experience the outfit as if you were trying it on in person.

        These outfit display options are designed to cater to user preferences and needs.

Users can explore how each option works in the videos provided below. Let's dive in!

Try On Outfits with "Wear"

Let's explore "Wear," the feature that lets users effortlessly try on outfits.

        Using the phone's camera, users can scan themselves and virtually try on outfits. just like having a fitting room in their pocket. It enhances confidence in their fashion choices and ensures a perfect fit.

A Secure and User-Friendly Scanning Experience

Users can scan themselves to virtually try on outfits.

  • Privacy Assurance: are provided with a privacy explanation prior to scanning to ensure their scans remain confidential. This builds trust and ensures user data is protected.
  • User-Friendly Guidance: Helpful tips on lighting, positioning, and movement make scanning a smooth and confident experience.

        This combination of privacy assurance and user-friendly guidance benefits both users and the business. Users can shop with confidence, and the business can provide a secure and enjoyable shopping experience, ultimately building trust and loyalty.

Your Mannequin, Your Style: Quick and Accessible Outfits for Everyone, Everywhere

        For users who face constraints like limited time or privacy concerns, the Mannequin option provides a quick way to visualize outfits.

  • Get a basic idea of how the outfit will look without a full body scan.

Empowering Choice: Your Data, Your Control

Users have full control over their data with the ability to easily retake or delete their scanned results and mannequin information

        It's all about empowering users with control over their virtual fitting experience.

Styling Made Simple: Enhancing Style with Accessories and Pairing

        Users often struggle with finding the right accessories for their outfits and feeling overwhelmed by too many options. To address this:

  • "Better Together" Option: VirtuFit simplifies the process with the "Better Together" option, allowing users to explore additional accessories that complement their outfit.
  • Visual Guidance: Plus icons on specific body parts serve as both visual reminders and interactive access points. Clicking on them shows users different items they can add in that area, making it easy for them to discover and decide on additional accessories.

        Additionally, when users try similar items or make outfit changes, VirtuFit suggests combinations that go well together, enhancing the overall styling experience.

Take Your customization to the Next Level

        Users may find it challenging to change specific items and may become overwhelmed by a large number of options, often leading to decision fatigue.

  • "Step-by-Step" Customization: Users can navigate through the selection process with ease, first choosing the main item (e.g., a shirt), then refining their choice by selecting the type (e.g., t-shirt), and finally, specifying the style (e.g., v-neck or lounge t-shirt).

  • Effective Filters: Filters are provided to help users narrow down their choices by attributes such as color, print, and sleeve length. This ensures a user-friendly experience and prevents overwhelming users with an abundance of options.

        This design prioritizes user-friendliness, offering a streamlined and enjoyable customization experience while preventing users from feeling overwhelmed by numerous choices.

Experience Matters: Building Trust with Virtual Fitting

       Let's start with a common challenge. Some users loved virtual fitting rooms because they accurately matched real products. But others felt let down because past apps often showed something different from what they bought. This made them worry.

  • Exploring Style from Every Angle:
    Utilizes a convenient carousel to display the chosen outfit from various angles, including side and back views, This allows user to evaluate how the outfit complements their personal style.

  • Boosting Confidence in Your Style:
    Next, I focused on boosting user confidence by highlighting outfit details and suitability for various occasions. This helps users feel more confident in their choices, even if the virtual representation isn't perfect.

  • Explore Your Fashion Choices:
    Allows users to get a closer look by clicking on items, opening product pages with multiple angles, detailed descriptions, reviews, and similar item suggestions, creating an informative shopping journey.

Inspired by User Worries: This decision drew inspiration from the concerns shared by users during my primary research, addressing their worries about virtual fitting rooms. The goal was to ensure that what users see on the screen aligns with what they receive, building confidence and enhancing the online shopping experience.

Build Your Digital Look Books: Saving and Sorting Outfit Inspirations

        I noticed that many users start their shopping journey with specific outfit inspirations in mind, whether it's a particular look, style, or outfit combination they've seen and wish to replicate. They desire a seamless way to bring these inspirations into their virtual wardrobe. To address this:

  • Your Outfit Inspirations Organized: Inspired by Instagram's "Save to Collection" feature, I designed a solution that optimizes the process of saving and organizing outfits. Now, user can build and manage digital look books, enabling you to curate and arrange your outfit inspirations
  • Streamlined Outfit Saving:On click 'Save' You can quickly save an outfit to your default wishlist. Furthermore, you have the flexibility to add the outfit to specific collections you've created in the past or on the go. It's a versatile tool that caters to your creativity and allows you to replicate or build upon your favorite looks with convenience.

        It's all about making it simpler for users to capture and arrange their fashion inspirations, creating a better overall experience.

Know What You're Paying For and Plan Your Outfit

Quickly view and manage outfits with an all-inclusive items list with concise descriptions and pricing of each item. The total cost adapts as users add or remove items, giving users a complete view of their outfit's makeup and expenses.

         This empowers users to make more informed and efficient purchase decisions, taking inspiration from the current "My Stylist" UI on Myntra.

Let's Explore the Transformation of the Outfit Building Screen

Before we uncover the transformation, Let's explore the screen...

Let's Explore the Transformation

          To improve the user experience, making it more practical, user-controlled, and efficient, I decided to make changes to this part of the feature.

          This made me rethink the user experience, especially because we were already having some concerns about using text prompts in the outfit builder. It was time to find a solution that could make both types of users happy – those who liked the predefined reasons and those who wanted to customize.

          Alright, so let's dive into how I designed the interface to accommodate these three different ways of providing prompts.

          After shifting the 'prompt input' to the Style Choice Selection Screen, However, this transition wasn't without its challenges due to the limitations I mentioned earlier. In my quest for a more user-friendly, practical, and efficient customisation solution, I explored various ideas and ultimately settled on one solution

          In the final version, the linear steps for practical customization remained, but a powerful addition was filters.

Users could now have more control in finding specific items and adjusting their outfit. Filters provided a convenient way to fine-tune their choices, making the outfit-building experience even more user-friendly.
